Top Papers

Radiosurgery Holds Its Ground
New England Journal of Medicine, Smith et al.
In this example randomized phase 3 trial, N=420 patients with brain metastases were assigned to stereotactic radiosurgery or whole-brain radiotherapy. Overall survival favored the focal approach with HR 0.82 (95% CI 0.70-0.96), while cognitive decline was lower. The practical lesson is familiar but important: control the disease without treating the entire brain like collateral damage.

Protons, This Time With Data
Journal of Clinical Oncology, Lee et al.
This example comparative study enrolled N=310 patients receiving proton therapy or IMRT for head and neck cancer. Grade 3 xerostomia was 9% versus 19% (p=0.01). It is not a blank cheque for particles, but it is the sort of toxicity signal that makes planning rounds more interesting.

The Trial Matcher Learns To Read
Nature Medicine, Nguyen et al.
A foundation model matched patients to clinical trials across 2,400 test cases with sensitivity 91% and specificity 84%. The important question is not whether the model can impress a benchmark, but whether it can reduce the quiet failure of eligible patients never being offered the right study.
